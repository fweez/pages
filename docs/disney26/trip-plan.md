### Notes
- Tickets include 2-day park admission, Lightning Lane Multi Pass both days, and Rise of the Resistance Single Pass for all 3 people
- Consider buying $300 in Disneyland food vouchers at 10% discount (~$270 net) — buy conservatively to avoid stranding unused value

- **Charging warning:** Top up in Red Bluff before heading east on Hwy 36 to Lassen — no reliable charging in the park

## Itinerary

### Day 1 — Saturday, June 6
**Portland → Mount Shasta | Drive: 6h**

Depart Portland in the morning. Arrive mid-afternoon with time to explore the area.

**Points of interest:**
- Mount Shasta itself — scenic views, short hikes around the base
- Castle Lake — short drive from town, beautiful alpine lake
- Downtown Mt. Shasta — small, charming mountain town with good food options

*Sleep: Mount Shasta (~$200)*

---

### Day 2 — Sunday, June 7
**Mount Shasta → Fresno | Drive: 6h45m**

Long Central Valley day — put on a good podcast. Natural lunch stop around Harris Ranch (halfway point, also a good charging stop).

**Points of interest:**
- Harris Ranch — classic roadside institution, good lunch stop, charging nearby

*Sleep: Fresno (~$120)*

---

### Day 3 — Monday, June 8
**Fresno → Anaheim + LA Tourism | Drive: 4h30m**

Arrive in the Anaheim/LA area by early afternoon, leaving time for sightseeing.

**Points of interest — LA area:**
- Santa Monica Pier and beach
- Griffith Observatory (great with kids, free admission)
- Hollywood Walk of Fame
- Venice Beach boardwalk

*Sleep: Anaheim ($550–$700 total for 3 nights)*

---

### Day 4 — Tuesday, June 9
**Disneyland Day 1**

Midweek — lighter crowds than weekends.

**Tips:**
- Scan into park early and immediately book first Lightning Lane reservation
- Rise of the Resistance: use Single Pass (pre-purchased) — go early, return times fill fast
- Book Lightning Lane for Indiana Jones Adventure early — sells out quickly
- Park hopping available after 11am

*Sleep: Anaheim*

---

### Day 5 — Wednesday, June 10
**Disneyland Day 2**

**Tips:**
- Use second day to hit anything missed on Day 1
- Check for parades and fireworks schedule — plan around them
- Disney California Adventure: Radiator Springs Racers requires Lightning Lane Single Pass (not included with Multi Pass)

*Sleep: Anaheim*

---

### Day 6 — Thursday, June 11
**Anaheim → Monterey | Drive: 7h**

Longest driving day of the return trip. Head up the coast.

**Points of interest — Monterey:**
- Monterey Bay Aquarium — excellent, great for kids, allow 3+ hours
- Cannery Row
- 17-Mile Drive (Pebble Beach area, scenic toll road)
- Carmel-by-the-Sea — walkable, charming, good food

*Sleep: Monterey ($175–$300)*

---

### Day 7 — Friday, June 12
**Monterey → Lassen National Park via Golden Gate Bridge | Drive: 6h30m**

Scenic return routing. Drive up through San Francisco, cross the Golden Gate Bridge, then north through the Central Valley and east on Hwy 36 into Lassen.

**Points of interest:**
- Golden Gate Bridge — stop at the north vista point in Marin for photos
- Muir Woods (30 min detour from bridge) — old-growth redwoods, very kid-friendly; reserve timed entry in advance at recreation.gov
- **Charging stop: Red Bluff** — last reliable charging before Lassen; top up here before heading east on Hwy 36

**Lassen National Park:**
- Sulphur Works — roadside hydrothermal area, easy walk, impressive
- Bumpass Hell trail (if time allows) — 3-mile round trip to largest hydrothermal area in park
- Manzanita Lake — beautiful, good evening walk around the lake

*Sleep: Lassen NP campground (~$40)*

---

### Day 8 — Saturday, June 13
**Lassen National Park → Portland | Drive: 9h**

Long drive home. Get an early start. Hwy 44 west to I-5, then north all the way.

**Morning at Lassen before departing:**
- Chaos Jumbles / Devastated Area — short roadside stops on the way out

---

## Alternate Plan: Days 7–8 via Mount Shasta

If the 9-hour final day feels too brutal, consider swapping Lassen for a second night in Mount Shasta:

| | Lassen Plan | Mt. Shasta Alternate |
|---|---|---|
| Day 7 drive | Monterey → Lassen (6h30m) | Monterey → Mt. Shasta (7h45m) |
| Day 8 drive | Lassen → Portland (9h) | Mt. Shasta → Portland (6h) |
| Day 7 longer? | No | +1h15m |
| Day 8 shorter? | — | Saves 3h |
| Camping? | Yes ($40) | No (~$200 hotel) |
| Scenery | Volcanic/hydrothermal | Mountain town (repeat of Day 1) |

**Verdict:** Mt. Shasta saves 3 hours on the last day at the cost of ~$160 more in lodging and a repeated stop. Worth considering if the 9-hour drive home sounds miserable after a full week of travel.
